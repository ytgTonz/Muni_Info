When to Call Emergency Services
URGENT - Call Immediately:
  • Life-threatening injuries
  • Heart attack, stroke
  • Serious accidents
  • House fires
  • Armed robbery
  • Gas leaks
Municipal Services:
  • Water pipe bursts
  • Power outages
  • Dangerous potholes
  • Sewage overflows
  • Illegal dumping
  • Street light failures
How to Make an Emergency Call
  1. Stay Calm - Take a deep breath and speak clearly
  2. Dial the Number - Use the appropriate emergency number
  3. Give Location - Provide exact address or landmarks
  4. Describe Emergency - What happened? How many people?
  5. Follow Instructions - Do what the operator tells you
  6. Stay on Line - Don't hang up until told to do so
Remember: Emergency calls are free from any phone, including mobile phones without airtime.
Emergency Preparedness Tips
At Home
  • • Keep emergency numbers visible
  • • Have a first aid kit
  • • Know where your water/gas shut-offs are
  • • Have flashlights and batteries
  • • Keep important documents safe
In Your Vehicle
  • • Keep your phone charged
  • • Have emergency supplies in car
  • • Know your exact location
  • • Have emergency contacts saved
  • • Keep spare tire inflated
For Your Family
  • • Teach children emergency numbers
  • • Have a family emergency plan
  • • Keep medical information updated
  • • Know allergies and medications
  • • Practice emergency procedures
